We present \textsc{SRE-Degrees}, a verifiable reinforcement-learning environment for
autonomous incident remediation in which the hard part of real outages is reproduced
faithfully: \emph{cascading} failures whose loudest alert points at a downstream victim
rather than the root cause, and where the naive fix makes the incident worse. The policy is
a \emph{frozen, swappable} LLM (no fine-tuning); reliability comes from the environment and a
\emph{root-cause-aware} reward that grades diagnosis, correct fix, and trap-avoidance rather
than ``did it come back up.'' We contribute (i) a Cascading-Incident Dependency-Graph (CIDG)
DSL with a deterministic in-process simulator and a high-fidelity live-cluster tier;
(ii) a graded reward with an explicit trap penalty that cleanly separates ``looks resolved''
from a clean win; and (iii) a \emph{searched} safety verifier --- a Thompson-sampling tree
over rules-as-data --- that learns a 3-rule safety harness which generalizes to held-out
incidents at 0.90 accuracy versus 0.95 hand-written. An honest fair-control ablation shows
the refinement loop's apparent lift is largely oracle-feedback leakage, relocating the
defensible contribution to the verifiable environment and the searched verifier. We release
51 generated scenarios, a deterministic judge, and FIREBALL-schema trajectories with genuine
within-group reward spread.
