\paragraph{One-shot diagnosis separates models.}
On one-shot diagnosis the environment lands in a 20--50\% reward band with real variance and
cleanly separates weak from strong policies (Table~\ref{tab:models}). Haiku scores
\(0.27\) mean against Opus at \(0.50\), with per-incident spread --- the within-group signal a
trainable reward needs.

\begin{table}[t]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lc}
\toprule
Frozen policy & Mean reward (one-shot) \\
\midrule
Claude Haiku 4.5   & 0.27 \\
Claude Opus 4.8    & 0.50 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{One-shot diagnosis reward, root-cause-aware grader.
Source: \texttt{rex/runs/hud\_eval\_showcase.log}.}
\label{tab:models}
\end{table}

\paragraph{The verifier generalizes.}
The Thompson-tree search, trained on 7 incidents, gates unsafe actions on 3 held-out incidents
it never saw at \(0.90\) accuracy versus \(0.95\) hand-written, while compressing
\(14 \rightarrow 3\) general rules with \emph{zero} synthesis-quality misses. The only
held-out miss is a hazard absent from training --- the search cannot invent an unseen failure
mode (Table~\ref{tab:verifier}).

\begin{table}[t]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
Verifier & Held-out acc. & \# rules \\
\midrule
Hand-written      & 0.95 & 14 \\
Searched (ours)   & 0.90 & 3  \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Searched verifier vs.\ hand-written on 3 held-out incidents.
Source: \texttt{rex/runs/harness\_synth.json} (HUD job abf124e2).}
\label{tab:verifier}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Open-model training.}
GRPO/RFT on Qwen3-8B and Qwen3-30B via the HUD Tinker provider is kicked off on the env's
graded reward; the first runs are flat on the easy tier (\(\sim\!0.5\), near ceiling) and a
longer run on the harder cascades (headroom \(\sim\!0.35\)) is underway
(\texttt{opensre-traj/train\_rft.py}).
