\section{Honest Ablation and Limitations}
\label{sec:ablation}

% [F5 content: ablation + limitations. Source: rex/runs/ablation.json, docs/headline_insights.md]
\paragraph{A fair control deflates the refinement loop.}
We ran a fair-control ablation isolating where the REx lift comes from. With the root-cause
hint stripped, REx collapses to \(0.25\), indistinguishable from zero-shot \(0.24\). Extra
compute (best-of-\(N\), \(0.24\)) and outcome-only feedback (retry, \(0.23\)) add essentially
nothing (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}). The apparent headline lift was largely
\emph{oracle-feedback leakage}.

\centering

\toprule
Condition & Mean reward \\
\midrule
Zero-shot                       & 0.24 \\
Best-of-$N$                     & 0.24 \\
Retry (outcome-only feedback)   & 0.23 \\
REx, root-cause hint stripped   & 0.25 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Fair-control ablation. Source: \texttt{rex/runs/ablation.json}.}
\label{tab:ablation}
\end{table}

\paragraph{What this re-centers.}
The defensible contributions are therefore the \emph{verifiable environment} and the
\emph{searched verifier}, not the refinement loop. We report this rather than the inflated
number.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
(i) The searched verifier cannot gate hazards absent from training. (ii) Tier-B fidelity is
bounded by the synthetic call mesh, not a production system of record. (iii) The mechanism
judge is an LLM and inherits its biases, mitigated but not eliminated by the
phrasing-robustness work. (iv) Open-model RFT results are preliminary.
