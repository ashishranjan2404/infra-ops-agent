# G7 — 10 Feedback for next task

For research/intel tasks, the highest-leverage move is to split the deliverable into a *cached snapshot*
(the cited brief) and a *re-runnable policy* (the structured watch-list with provenance + a `verifiability`
field), so the honesty about marketing-vs-fact lives in the schema rather than in prose a reader can skim
past. Dual-source every dated, load-bearing claim (funding/valuation) and tag all self-reported metrics
`(vendor-reported)` with the customer named — reviewers discount un-attributed numbers to zero. A tiny
validator script turns "I wrote some YAML" into "this artifact provably conforms," which is cheap proof of
non-placeholder work. Biggest gap to close next time: a competitor brief is only valuable as a *time series*,
so a follow-up should actually wire the watch-list into a scheduled re-poll and add a competitive matrix
against the other AI-SRE entrants rather than profiling one vendor in isolation.
