# G3 — SREGym pass@1 leaderboard positioning: where would we rank?

**Question:** If we dropped our agent onto SREGym's published pass@1 (E2E) leaderboard,
where would we land? **Short answer:** on a *fair single-attempt* basis we are a
**bottom-of-board entry (rank 8 of 13 in the merged view, ~34.9%)**, above only the two
weakest leaderboard rows and below every frontier-model agent. Our REx number (89.7%)
would nominally "top" the table, but it is **out-of-regime** (multi-attempt + oracle hint)
and its placement is a category error, not a win.

---

## 1. SREGym's published numbers (cited)

Source: SREGym paper **arXiv:2605.07161v1** and the public leaderboard
**sregym.com/leaderboard**, retrieved 2026-06-25 (frozen in `sregym_reported.json`).

- **Benchmark:** 90 problems on **live containerized (Kubernetes) microservice apps**
  with injected high-fidelity faults; partitions ported(34)/similar(43)/new(13).
- **Metric:** "success rate" over 3 runs, **1 attempt per run** -> this is pass@1
  semantics even though the paper does not use the "pass@1" label.
- **Grader:** Diagnosis = checklist LLM-judge on fault localization; Mitigation = live
  oracle verifying the real system recovered; **E2E = correct diagnosis AND mitigation
  on the same run**.

Published E2E (no-noise unless marked), top to bottom:

| Codex (GPT-5.4, noise) | 45.9% |

## 3. The merged ranked leaderboard

See `ranked_leaderboard.md` (generated by `rank_leaderboard.py`). Headline placement:

- **Fair band (best_of_n / retry_realistic ~34.9%): rank 8 of 13.** Above only Kimi-K2.5
  Stratus (32.9% / 30.4%); below every Sonnet-4.6 / GPT-5.4 agent. Conclusion holds for
  A2 too (our fair band is even lower there, ~31%).
- **REx (89.7%): nominal rank 1 — but OUT-OF-REGIME.** REx spends multiple attempts AND
  a P0 oracle hint that no SREGym agent receives. A1's own ablation shows rex_no_oracle
  collapses to ~33%: the tree alone buys little; the oracle does the work. So REx topping
  the board is a category error, not a SREGym win.

## 4. Honest positioning

On a like-for-like single-attempt basis **we do not lead SREGym** — our strongest fair
condition lands in the lower part of their E2E band, beating only their weakest
(Kimi-K2.5) entries and trailing all frontier-model agents. That is the honest headline.
The one place we "win" (REx) is in a regime SREGym does not measure. Both benchmarks
independently agree that **novel/unseen failures are hardest**: SREGym E2E collapses
ported->new (Claude Code 60.8->28.2, Stratus 63.7->17.9, Codex 57.8->15.4), and our
zero-shot cascade pass@1 is just 6.7%.

## 5. Non-equivalence caveats (why "rank" is NOT apples-to-apples)

1. **Substrate:** SREGym = live Kubernetes microservices; ours = deterministic simulator.
2. **Grader:** SREGym = checklist judge + live recovery oracle; ours = reward@0.8 threshold
   (a pass is *cheaper to earn* in our setup).
3. **Task overlap:** ZERO shared incidents; different fault catalogs entirely.
4. **Metric decomposition:** SREGym E2E = diagnosis AND mitigation; our reward is a single
   scalar, not a diagnosis x mitigation product — they don't decompose the same way.
5. **Attempt budget:** SREGym = 1 attempt/run; our REx = many; only our zero_shot /
   best_of_n / retry / rex_no_oracle are single-final-answer analogues.
6. **Oracle feedback:** our REx receives a P0 oracle hint with no SREGym counterpart.
7. **Model mismatch:** ours = glm-5p2 / deepseek-v4-pro (cheaper); SREGym top rows =
   Sonnet 4.6 / GPT-5.4 (frontier). The model gap alone could explain the deficit.
8. **Noise axis:** SREGym tests no-noise AND noise (agents degrade under noise);
   we have NO noise axis — we only tested the easy case.
9. **Threshold knob:** our pass depends on the 0.8 reward cutoff; moving it moves our rank.
10. **n / CI asymmetry:** SREGym n=90 with no published per-row CIs; ours n=126/150 with
    Wilson CIs — we cannot run a cross-benchmark significance test (we lack their
    per-problem outcomes), so no "we are significantly above/below row X" claim is made.
11. **Family<->partition mapping** between our simple/cascade/novel and SREGym
    ported/similar/new is loose and illustrative, not a registered correspondence.

## 6. Bottom line
Where would we rank? **Fairly: near the bottom of the SREGym E2E board (~34.9%, above
only Kimi-K2.5), behind all frontier agents.** The honest contribution is not a leaderboard
win — it is (a) a reproducible single-attempt baseline that is *credibly in the same
ballpark's lower band* despite cheaper models and a different substrate, and (b) a REx
lift that is real but measured in a regime SREGym does not score, so it must not be sold
as a SREGym #1.
