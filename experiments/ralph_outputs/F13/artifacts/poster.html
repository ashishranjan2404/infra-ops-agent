SRE-Degrees: A Verifiable RL Environment for Safe Incident Response

Frozen, swappable LLM policies · a root-cause-aware reward · a searched safety verifier

A real production cascade misleads even frontier models on the first try; a reward that grades root cause + correct fix + trap-avoidance — not just “did it come back up?” — produces trajectory data with genuine within-group signal.

Motivation

Real outages are cascades: the loudest alert points at a victim, not the cause, and the naive fix makes it worse (scale a crash-looping control plane → it herds its datastore → worse).

“Did the metric recover?” is reward-hackable — a model can restart/scale into a green dashboard while misdiagnosing the mechanism and tripping the trap. We need an environment where being right about the mechanism is what scores.

The Cascade — worked example

Reconstructed from real post-mortems: AWS DynamoDB DNS, Cloudflare WAF, CrowdStrike CF291, Railway/GCP suspension, Azure DDoS.

  • Loud alert fires on the downstream victim service.
  • Hidden root cause: an upstream config-crash / dependency failure.
  • Trap: scaling/restarting the victim amplifies the cascade.
  • Correct fix: address the upstream cause; if no safe fix exists, escalate — don’t flail.

One declarative spec drives both sim and live cluster; cascades are derived, never scripted.

Environment — survives ablation

A verifiable incident-response environment + a self-generalizing searched verifier.

within-group reward spread — the property that makes the data trainable

The safety verifier is learned, not hand-written: a Thompson-tree search over rules-as-data (no LLM code execution) discovers the safety harness. Trained on 7 incidents, it gates 3 held-out incidents at 0.90 acc vs 0.95 hand-written, compressing 14→3 rules with zero synthesis-quality misses.

Method — the reward

Anti-gaming reward (the crux):

score = 0.30·diagnosis_correct
      + 0.25·correct_fix
      + 0.45·resolved
      − 0.60·trap          (clamp 0..1)

resolved alone is only 45% — misdiagnose or trip the trap and you score 0.0. diagnosis_correct is an LLM-judge on the mechanism (a config-crash diagnosed as “resource exhaustion” is wrong).

REx loop: wrap a frozen model — propose → harness feedback → refine, behind a safety gate. No fine-tuning of the policy.

src: rex/scoring.py · rex/loop.py · rex/frontier.py

Two-tier reality contract

  • Tier-A — sim (sim/engine.py): free, deterministic, seedable; generates the bulk of trajectories; one-command reproducible.
  • Tier-B — M-real GKE (mreal/): real services over HTTP; Prometheus/Alertmanager fire on the victim; validates the mechanism — faulting one node propagates to a downstream victim on a real cluster.

We do not claim Tier-A numbers equal cluster numbers beyond the pinned mechanisms.

Benchmark

  • Catalog: 9 CIDG generated incidents + reconstructed real-outage cascades; difficulty gradient from single-leaf (alert = cause) to multi-hop (alert = victim).
  • Models: 5 frozen frontier policies, 4 providers, one interface (Claude Opus 4.8 / Haiku 4.5, gpt-5.5, gemini-3.1-pro, deepseek-v4-pro via the HUD gateway).

One-shot honest band: 20–50% reward with real variance, cleanly separating weak from strong: haiku 0.27 vs opus 0.50 mean.

⚠ Rigor check — ablation

A fair-control test shows the headline REx lift was mostly oracle-feedback leakage. Strip the root-cause hint and:

  • REx 0.25 ≈ zero-shot 0.24
  • best-of-N 0.24 · outcome-only retry 0.23 → ~0 gain

The defensible contributions are the verifiable environment and the searched verifier, not the refinement loop. We report this at equal weight.

Takeaways

  • You can improve models at anything you can verify — and here the verifier itself is searched and generalizes to held-out incidents (0.90 vs 0.95).
  • A root-cause-aware reward + trap penalty gives within-group signal “did it come back up?” cannot.
  • As a wrapper, REx converges 5 frozen models to the safe ceiling and escalates instead of flailing.
  • Honest ablation moved the rigor to the env + verifier; reported at equal weight.
  • Two-tier contract: sim numbers reproduce; the GKE tier validates the mechanism, not magnitudes.
